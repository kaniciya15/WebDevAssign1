Introduction

Sea lion are part of the group the semi-aquatic marine mammels. It is also known as Pinnipeds. There lifespan is around 20-30 years unless they are affected badly by parasites or diseases.

The male weighs 300kg and is of length 2.4m and the female weighs 100kg and is of length 1.8m. Sea lions can swim in the speed of 30mph. The endangered species of sea lions live in Australia and New Zealand.

Parasite & Diseases

Parasite-Sea lions get affected by both internal and external parsites Internal-Lungworm affects their respiratory system,heart,liver and stomach External-Lice and ticks are most common ones.

Disease-Sea lions all around the world get affected by the disease called Leptospirosis, which is a bacterial infection and it affects the kidneys of the sea lion causing them an abdominal pain and even more sometimes death also could occur.

Interaction with humans

Sea lions are not dangerous to humans but it is always good to maintain a distance because there are some injuries caused by them to humans.

Even though they look so sweet and nice we shouldn't pet, feed or even swim with them,since they are wild animals you can't predict their actions.

Traning the sea lions

Always we should ensure a positive environment around the animal and start with baby steps and by giving them the reward(food) for every correct task they complete. By using voice signal, hand signal and the whistle we can guide the animal correctly.Using all these way the trainers are able to train the sea lions for the show that we all enjoy.
